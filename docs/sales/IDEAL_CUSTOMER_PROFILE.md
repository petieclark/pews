# Pews Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Overview

Not every church is a great fit for Pews. This document defines our **ideal customer**—the churches most likely to benefit from Pews, convert quickly, stay long-term, and refer others.

---

## Sweet Spot: Church Size

### Ideal: 100-500 Active Members
- Large enough to need real software (spreadsheets break down)
- Small enough to appreciate simplicity and personal support
- Budget-conscious but willing to invest in good tools

### Also Good: 50-100 Members
- Growing fast and need to scale systems before chaos hits
- Transitioning from paper/spreadsheets

### Also Good: 500-1,500 Members
- Frustrated with high costs of Planning Center + Tithe.ly + Mailchimp
- Need consolidation and cost savings
- May have 1-2 staff members managing systems

### Not Ideal (Yet): Under 50 Members
- Often can't justify $100/mo (even though it's a bargain)
- May not need all features yet (check-in, groups, etc.)
- Better served by free tools (spreadsheets, Google Forms) until they grow

### Not Ideal (Yet): Over 2,000 Members
- Often need enterprise-level customization, SLAs, dedicated support
- May have complex integrations or denominational reporting requirements
- We can handle them, but it's not our strength yet

## Pain Points That Indicate Readiness

### 🔥 Hot Signals (Immediate Need)

**"We're paying too much for church software."**
- Currently using 3+ tools (PCO, Tithe.ly, Mailchimp, etc.)
- Spending $300-800/mo
- Leadership is price-sensitive (asking for budget cuts)

**"We're copying data between systems."**
- Adding new people to PCO, then manually adding to Mailchimp
- Reconciling giving data with accounting software
- Wasting hours on manual data entry

**"Our current tool is frustrating."**
- Staff/volunteers complain about complexity
- Check-in is slow or unreliable
- Giving reports are hard to generate
- Can't get support when they need it

**"We just hired/lost our admin, and the new person is overwhelmed."**
- Knowledge is locked in one person's head
- New admin needs simpler tools
- Fresh eyes = opportunity to re-evaluate

**"We're launching a new campus/service and need better systems."**
- Growth phase = willingness to invest in infrastructure
- Current tools won't scale
- Leadership is already thinking about systems

---

### ⚠️ Warm Signals (Exploring, Not Urgent)

**"We've been using [old tool] for years, but wondering if there's something better."**
- Not in crisis, but open to change
- Curious about modern alternatives
- May need education on cost savings

**"We're a church plant planning to launch in 6-12 months."**
- Need to build systems from scratch
- Budget-conscious (often bi-vocational pastor)
- Want simple, scalable tools

**"We're tired of paper check-in / spreadsheets."**
- Recognizing they've outgrown manual processes
- Ready to invest in real software
- May not know what's available

**"Our pastor/board is asking for better data."**
- Leadership wants dashboards, reports, trends
- Current tools don't provide insights
- Opportunity to position Pews as "data-driven ministry"

---

### 🚫 Red Flags (Disqualify or Deprioritize)

**"We need this to be free."**
- Unlikely to convert (even at $100/mo)
- May churn as soon as they find something cheaper
- Not a sustainable customer

**"We need on-premise hosting for security reasons."**
- Pews is cloud-only
- Wrong product for their requirements

**"We need [complex integration] that Pews doesn't support yet."**
- Examples: ShelbyNext sync, denomination-specific reporting
- Set expectations: "We're building that, but it's not ready yet."

**"We just signed a 3-year contract with Planning Center."**
- Sunk cost is too high
- Revisit in 2-3 years

**"We're price shopping and just want the cheapest option."**
- Will churn the moment someone undercuts us
- High maintenance, low lifetime value

**"Our IT committee requires [X obscure compliance thing]."**
- May have requirements Pews can't meet yet (e.g., HIPAA for counseling records)
- If blocker, disqualify gracefully

## Decision Maker Roles

### Who Influences the Decision?

| Role | Influence Level | What They Care About |
|------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Lead Pastor** | High | Ease of use, cost savings, staff happiness, data/insights |
| **Executive Pastor** | Very High | Operations, efficiency, ROI, consolidation |
| **Admin / Office Manager** | Very High | Day-to-day usability, support, learning curve |
| **Worship Pastor** | Medium | (Only if they manage volunteers or check-in) |
| **Board / Elders** | Medium-High | Cost, risk, long-term stability |
| **IT Volunteer** | Low-Medium | Security, integrations, technical feasibility |

### Decision-Making Dynamics

**Small Churches (50-200 members)**:
- Lead pastor often makes the call (sometimes solo, sometimes with board input)
- May involve admin if one exists
- Decision cycle: 1-4 weeks

**Mid-Size Churches (200-800 members)**:
- Executive pastor or admin is primary decision-maker
- Lead pastor signs off
- May need board approval for budget
- Decision cycle: 2-8 weeks

**Large Churches (800+ members)**:
- Operations/admin team evaluates and recommends
- Executive pastor or CFO approves budget
- Lead pastor may or may not be involved
- Decision cycle: 4-12 weeks (longer if committees are involved)

## Buying Timeline

### Immediate (Ready to Buy in 1-2 Weeks)
**Trigger Events**:
- Current tool just raised prices
- Contract just expired
- New admin just started and hates current tool
- Critical failure (e.g., check-in crashed on Sunday)

**Sales Approach**:
- Fast demo, fast proposal, fast onboarding
- Prioritize speed and ease

---

### Short-Term (1-3 Months)
**Trigger Events**:
- Budget planning season (often October-December for Jan 1 fiscal year)
- Upcoming launch (new campus, new service)
- Pastor/board expressed dissatisfaction with current tools

**Sales Approach**:
- Demo, trial, comparison docs
- Give them time to evaluate and build internal consensus
- Stay in touch weekly

---

### Long-Term (3-12 Months)
**Trigger Events**:
- Church plant planning phase
- Multi-year contract ending in 6+ months
- "Kicking tires" but no immediate pain

**Sales Approach**:
- Nurture via email sequences
- Check in monthly
- Offer to revisit when timing is right

---

### Not Ready (12+ Months)
**Examples**:
- Church plant in idea phase (no launch date)
- Locked in long-term contract with no exit clause
- "Just exploring options" with no budget or timeline

**Sales Approach**:
- Add to email list for quarterly updates
- Don't spend time on active sales (low conversion probability)

## Firmographic Filters

### Geography (For Now)
**Best Fit**: United States
- Pews is built for US churches (tax statements, giving compliance, etc.)
- Support is US time zones
- Payment processing is USD

**Possible Fit**: Canada, English-speaking countries
- Can work, but may lack localized features (e.g., Canadian tax receipts)

**Not Ready**: Non-English-speaking countries
- UI is English-only (for now)
- Localization (language, currency, compliance) not built yet

---

### Denomination
**All denominations welcome.**
- Pews is non-denominational by design
- Works for Baptist, Methodist, Presbyterian, non-denominational, Pentecostal, etc.

**Caveat**: Some denominations require specific reporting (e.g., Catholic diocesan reports, SBC annual church profile). If a church has hard requirements we can't meet yet, set expectations or disqualify.

---

### Church Type
**Best Fit**:
- **Community churches** (weekend services, kids programming, small groups, volunteers)
- **Church plants** (need simple, affordable systems from day one)
- **Multi-site churches (2-5 campuses)** (need consolidation and cost savings)

**Also Good**:
- **College/young adult ministries** (engagement tracking, events)
- **House church networks** (lightweight people management)

**Not Ideal**:
- **Megachurches (5,000+ members)** (may need enterprise-level customization)
- **Catholic parishes** (different structure, different needs—may require diocesan reporting)
- **Niche ministries** (e.g., prison ministry, campus ministry) where Pews features don't align

## Psychographic Traits

### Values
**Ideal customers value**:
- **Simplicity** over complexity (don't want bloated software)
- **Cost savings** (stewarding resources well)
- **Consolidation** (fewer tools = less headache)
- **Transparency** (clear pricing, no hidden fees)
- **Support** (want real humans to help when stuck)

### Attitudes Toward Technology
**Best Fit**:
- **Pragmatic adopters**: Not bleeding-edge, but willing to try new tools if they solve real problems
- **Frustrated with current tools**: Open to change because status quo isn't working
- **Budget-conscious**: Willing to switch to save money

**Not Ideal**:
- **Luddites**: "We've always done it this way" (paper forever)
- **Bleeding-edge early adopters**: Want the newest, shiniest thing (Pews is practical, not flashy)
- **Enterprise buyers**: Expect white-glove service, custom contracts, SLAs

## Disqualification Criteria

### Hard Disqualifiers (Can't Serve Them Yet)
- **Requires on-premise hosting** (Pews is cloud-only)
- **Requires multi-language support** (English-only for now)
- **Requires denominational integrations** we don't support (e.g., SBC ACP sync)
- **Has security/compliance requirements** we can't meet (e.g., HIPAA for counseling records)

### Soft Disqualifiers (Not Worth the Effort)
- **Chronic price shoppers** (will churn for $10/mo savings)
- **Abusive or unkind** (life's too short)
- **Locked in long-term contracts** with no exit (revisit in 1-2 years)
- **Under 30 members** (unless growing fast or willing to invest ahead of growth)

## Ideal Customer Narrative

### Who They Are
Grace Community Church is a 280-member church in a suburb of a mid-size city. They have:
- 1 lead pastor (full-time)
- 1 part-time admin (20 hours/week)
- 15-20 volunteers (nursery, kids, greeting, production)
- Sunday attendance: ~180-200
- 2 services (9 AM, 11 AM)
- Kids programming (nursery through 5th grade)
- 8-10 small groups
- Monthly giving: ~$28,000

### Their Pain
They're currently using:
- **Planning Center People + Check-Ins**: $120/mo
- **Tithe.ly**: $69/mo + 2.9% transaction fees
- **Mailchimp**: $45/mo (350 contacts)
- **Total**: ~$234/mo + giving fees

Their admin, Sarah, is frustrated because:
- She has to manually copy new visitors from PCO to Mailchimp
- Giving reports from Tithe.ly don't match PCO attendance data
- Check-in labels jam frequently (old printer)
- The pastor wants better engagement data, but it's scattered across tools

### Why Pews is Perfect
- **Cost savings**: $100/mo (vs. $234) = $1,608/year saved
- **Consolidation**: One tool instead of three
- **Simplicity**: Sarah can manage everything herself (no IT committee needed)
- **Better data**: Unified dashboard shows attendance + giving + engagement in one place

### Their Journey
1. **Week 1**: Sarah Googles "church management software cheaper than Planning Center" → finds Pews
2. **Week 1**: Fills out demo request form → gets 30-min demo
3. **Week 2**: Shows pastor and board the cost comparison → gets approval
4. **Week 3**: Signs up, starts data migration
5. **Week 4**: Goes live on Sunday → check-in works flawlessly
6. **Week 5**: Sarah spends 3 hours less per week on admin tasks
7. **Month 2**: Pastor tells neighboring church about Pews → referral

## Questions to Ask During Discovery

Use these to qualify/disqualify quickly:

1. **"How many active members do you have?"** (Size filter)
2. **"What tools are you currently using?"** (Pain point discovery)
3. **"How much are you paying per month?"** (Cost savings opportunity)
4. **"What's frustrating about your current setup?"** (Pain amplification)
5. **"Who makes the decision to switch tools?"** (Decision-maker identification)
6. **"What's your timeline for deciding?"** (Urgency assessment)
7. **"What would make this a no-brainer for you?"** (Close path clarity)

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- "We need to run this by our IT committee" (may slow down or kill deal)
- "We're just starting to look" (low urgency, long sales cycle)
- "We need custom integrations with [obscure tool]" (may be unsupported)
- "What's your lowest price?" (price shopper, will churn)
- "Can you come present to our board?" (long sales cycle, low close rate)
